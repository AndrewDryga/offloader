    How this is computed

    Honest by construction

    Reducible, not total

    Only bounded, endpoint-mapped spend counts as reducible — the repeated query shapes Offloader can serve from a snapshot. Ad-hoc joins and one-off exports stay on the warehouse and are excluded. Net savings = reducible × a reduction factor − Offloader's monthly cost.

    Confidence levels

    1. 1 — the repeated workload is identified (a technical candidate, no dollar claim).
    2. 2 — query logs + cost allocation estimate the reducible spend. This tool tops out here.
    3. 3 — warehouse size or schedule can actually be reduced.
    4. 4 — finance verifies the bill dropped, after you offload.

    Three scenarios

    Reduction of the reducible spend is modeled at 40% conservative, 70% expected, and 90% aggressive. The expected row also drives the payback estimate on your one-time migration labor.

    Where it won't overclaim

    On committed capacity the savings show as potential, not realized with no payback — cutting query volume doesn't lower a flat-rate bill until you resize. A shared warehouse adds an attribution caveat. It never promises a bill reduction the inputs don't support.
